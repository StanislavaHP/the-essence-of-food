Pomegranates

The pomegranate is a fruit. It originated in the region between Iran and northern India. Rich in vitamin K, it is often consumed as a snack or used as a garnish.

Fun facts about the Pomegranates

The pomegranate is a shrub or a small tree. It has surprisingly long life span (some are known to have lived 200 years).
The name directly translated means seeded apple.
India and Iran are the largest producers of pomegranate.
The seeds of pomegranates are used to produce oil.
