Asparagus

The asparagus is a vegetable which originated in Europe and Western Asia. Rich in vitamin K and folate, it is often consumed as a side dish grilled or baked.

Fun facts about the Asparagus

The expression "faster than cooking asparagus" is attributed to Emperor Augustus who created the "Asparagus Fleet" tasked with harvesting the vegetable.

Once the buds of the asparagus start to open, the vegetable quickly turns woody and is not suitable for consumption.

China is the world's largest producer by significant margin.

White asparagus are white because they are not exposed to sunlight.
